This is more than an academic transition; it is the story of a soul's reawakening. It traces the path of a high school humanities devotee—steeped in history and politics—who, against all odds, heard a distant, compelling call from the world of pure abstraction. This is a record of that rebellion, the struggles that followed, and the unwavering resolve that was forged in the fire of mathematics.

The Reluctant Beginning: A Rebel's First Step

09/2022

I stood at the university gate, a former humanities student thousands of kilometers from home, filled with doubt about mathematics. My father, a computer science Ph.D., walked beside me—his presence both comforting and daunting. Little did I know that behind these gates awaited a revolution that would shatter and rebuild my entire worldview.

The Strategic Gambit: Confronting My Weakness

12/2022

In a bold move, I chose to teach the very subject that terrified me: Linear Algebra. The night before my first lecture, anxiety nearly consumed me. But as I deconstructed concepts for my peers, a miracle occurred—the fog lifted, revealing breathtaking patterns. This wasn't just teaching; it was my mathematical awakening, where I discovered an innate affinity for algebraic structures that would redirect my entire academic trajectory.

The Crossroads: Physics or Mathematics?

06/2023

Surrounded by brilliant physicists at the astronomy conference, I felt like an imposter in borrowed robes. While others discussed cosmic phenomena, my mind kept tracing the mathematical structures underlying them. That night, under a starry sky, I made the most difficult decision of my academic life: to abandon the security of applied research and pursue the treacherous, beautiful path of pure mathematics.

The Revelation: Falling in Love with Abstraction

12/2023

The moment I understood Galois theory, I felt I had discovered a secret language of the universe. While peers struggled with abstraction, I found myself intoxicated by it. From Advanced Linear Algebra to Commutative Algebra, each lecture felt like unveiling a masterpiece. When a student asked about the "practical use" of unsolvable quintics, I smiled: "Some beauty needs no justification." In that moment, I knew I had become a mathematician.

The Mirror of Solitude: Zhang Yitang's Lesson

07/2024

Listening to Professor Zhang recount his decades of solitary pursuit, I saw my future—and it terrified me. As he described working in isolation, driven only by mathematical intuition, I remembered my own lonely nights plotting prime number spirals. His story wasn't just inspirational; it was a warning. Mathematics demands everything—your time, your relationships, your very identity. Walking back that evening, the weight of this realization settled upon me.

The Rebellion: Building a Sanctuary

01/2025

If the path didn't exist, I would build it myself. Frustrated by the lack of pure mathematics community, I created one. Sending those first invitation emails felt more daunting than any exam. But as the room filled with curious faces, I realized this was my answer to the solitude. We weren't just sharing knowledge; we were building a sanctuary for those who saw beauty in abstraction.

The Epiphany: Mathematics as Relationship

03/2025

When I discovered category theory—"the study of mathematical objects through their relationships rather than their internal structure"—I felt I had come home. This was the mathematics I had always dreamed of! My previous understanding of group homomorphisms as "bridges" was merely a glimpse of categories and functors. And when I encountered the Yoneda Lemma—"an object is determined by its relationships with all others"—it felt less like mathematics and more like philosophy: reality is defined by interaction, not isolation.

The Cycle: From Apprentice to Guide

04/2025

My journey from student to Algebra Teaching Assistant completed a beautiful cycle. Every week at the Help Center, I watched younger students wrestle with concepts I had once struggled with. Designing challenging problems and seeing the spark in their eyes when they solved them brought me full circle—I was now paying forward the guidance that had shaped my own path.

The Awakening: Westlake University Summer School

07/2025

Surrounded by brilliant minds at Westlake University, my mathematical passion exploded with new intensity. Discussions with Professors Huayi Chen and Xing Gu about homotopy theory and academic futures opened horizons I hadn't imagined. In that environment of intellectual ferment, I understood: true growth requires venturing beyond familiar borders into the broader mathematical world.

The Convergence: Visiting Scholar at Westlake

09/2025

As a visiting scholar in Professor Gu's homotopy theory group, while simultaneously diving into categorification and Lean, everything clicked into place. I could swiftly grasp algebraic topology's abstractions, structuring topological ideas with algebraic language. Yet with this clarity came sobering awareness: my knowledge had limits, and my ambition had none. I knew then that I must reach for the highest platforms mathematics has to offer.

The Road Ahead

I am still on the path, and I will always be.
Looking back, I see that my journey was shaped by fate, by choice, and by the future self I am determined to become.
This is only the prologue—the opening chapter of my odyssey in pure mathematics.

Maybe not today, maybe not tomorrow.
But one truth remains unshakable:
I will become a pure mathematician—a person defined and completed by this pursuit.

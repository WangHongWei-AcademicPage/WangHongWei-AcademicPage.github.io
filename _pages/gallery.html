This is not a typical success story. This is a confession—a record of how a humanities student who once wandered through history and politics found himself unexpectedly captivated by the absolute beauty of pure mathematics, embarking on a path of no return.

The Beginning: A Rebel's Reluctant First Step

09/2022

❝ I remember standing at the university gate, filled with uncertainty. Having suspended my studies for a year and immersed in the humanities, mathematics felt like a foreign language to me. My father, a computer science Ph.D., walked beside me—his presence both comforting and intimidating. "Maybe I'll just get through this," I thought, never imagining that behind these gates awaited a revolution that would shatter and rebuild my entire worldview.

The Turning Point: Facing My Greatest Fear

12/2022

❝ They say you never truly understand something until you teach it. So I chose to teach the one subject that terrified me most: Linear Algebra. The night before my first lecture, I almost backed out. "What if I fail? What if they see through my facade?" But as I stood before my peers, explaining row reduction and vector spaces, something miraculous happened—the fog began to lift. In the process of deconstructing knowledge for others, I discovered a profound truth: the elegance of algebraic structures spoke to me in a way no historical narrative ever had. This wasn't just teaching; this was my mathematical awakening.

The Crossroads: Physics or Mathematics?

06/2023

❝ Standing at the astronomy conference, surrounded by brilliant physicists, I felt like an imposter. Professor Pang had given me everything—Python skills, research training, a path forward. Yet as I listened to talks about cosmic phenomena, my mind kept wandering back to the mathematical structures underlying them. The equations fascinated me more than their physical interpretations. That night, staring at the starry sky, I made the most difficult decision of my academic life: to abandon the security of applied research and pursue the treacherous path of pure mathematics. I was choosing beauty over utility, abstraction over application.

The Revelation: Falling in Love with Abstraction

12/2023

❝ The moment I understood Galois theory, I felt like I had discovered a secret language of the universe. While my peers struggled with the abstraction, I found myself intoxicated by it. Teaching commutative algebra became not just a duty, but a joy. Each lecture felt like unveiling a masterpiece—group theory's symmetry, field extensions' elegance, the heartbreaking genius of Galois' short life. One evening, after explaining the unsolvability of the quintic, a student asked, "But what's the practical use of this?" I smiled and replied, "Some beauty needs no justification." In that moment, I knew: I was no longer studying mathematics; I had become a mathematician.

The Solitude: Zhang Yitang's Mirror

07/2024

❝ Listening to Professor Zhang recount his decades of solitary pursuit, I saw my future—and it terrified me. As he described working in isolation, driven only by mathematical intuition, I remembered my own lonely nights plotting prime number spirals, searching for patterns that might not even exist. His story wasn't just inspirational; it was a warning. Mathematics demands everything—your time, your relationships, your very identity. Walking back to my dorm, the weight of this realization crushed me. Was I prepared for such solitude? Did I possess that kind of courage? The prime numbers on my screen seemed to mock my uncertainty.

The Rebellion: Creating What Was Missing

01/2025

❝ If the path didn't exist, I would build it myself. Frustrated by the lack of pure mathematics community, I decided to create one. Sending those first invitation emails felt more daunting than any exam. "Who would come? Was I qualified to lead this?" But as the room filled with curious faces, something shifted. Standing there, explaining Galois' genius to students from different backgrounds, I realized this was my answer to the solitude. We weren't just sharing knowledge; we were building a sanctuary for those who saw beauty in abstraction. Looking at the engaged audience, I understood: my journey was no longer about personal achievement, but about creating spaces where mathematical beauty could flourish.

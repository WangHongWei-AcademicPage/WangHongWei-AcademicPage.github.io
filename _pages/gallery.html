This gallery chronicles my mathematical journey since the beginning of university. From a high school humanities student—who suspended studies for a year and later focused on politics, history, and geography—to now standing at the crossroads of life as a final-year student, unwavering and carrying the dream of becoming a pure mathematician. University has offered me more than academic growth; it has been a quest to discover a lifelong passion and innate calling. These moments are preserved here: this is my path in pure mathematics—a path of purity.

A journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step

09/2022

My father accompanied me to XJTLU, which is thousands of kilometers away from our hometown, to help me settle in. As a Ph.D. in Computer Science, he has always been a great source of academic guidance and inspiration for me.

"The best way to truly understand a problem is to explain it clearly to others"

12/2022

A strategic decision during my freshman year proved transformative: as a new student lecturer, I chose to teach Linear Algebra precisely because it was my weaker subject, forcing me to master it thoroughly. The review series I subsequently created was met with widespread student praise and became the most-watched content on the Math Society's channel. More importantly, this process of teaching and deep engagement with the material cemented my affinity for algebraic structures, fundamentally directing my academic trajectory toward algebra.

Astronomy Conference at Sun Yat-sen University

06/2023

Under Professor Pang's mentorship, I acquired foundational Python programming skills during my freshman year. As a member of the research group, I received systematic research guidance and training, which laid the groundwork for my future path in research. It was also during this period that I profoundly realized the fundamental distinction between physics and mathematics.

Mathematics is the structure of universe

12/2023

As I delved deeper into my undergraduate mathematics studies, I increasingly found that, compared to the rigorous mystique of analysis, I was more drawn to the elegance and structured nature of algebra. Gradually, I embarked on a path dedicated to pure mathematics with a focus on algebra. Serving as the student lecturer for algebra at my university, I continually refined my understanding through teaching. From Advanced Linear Algebra and Abstract Algebra to Commutative Algebra and Galois Theory, my direction in pure mathematics crystallized, and I came to a profound realization: my goal is to become a pure mathematician.

'Mathematics is an arduous and solitary journey.'

07/2024

During my research on large-scale prime number visualization under Professor Pietro, I had the privilege of attending a lecture by Professor Yitang Zhang on his landmark work regarding the bounded gaps of prime numbers. He recounted his decades-long solitary pursuit—how he withdrew from the world, driven by an almost obsessive conviction and the purest intuition for mathematics, until he finally overcame this millennia-old challenge. His story served as a mirror, reflecting my own experience: those days and nights I spent plotting prime spirals and searching for their macroscopic patterns made me feel the same insignificance and solitude one faces before the vast universe of mathematics. At that moment, I came to a profound realization—from Professor Zhang's extraordinary solitary courage, to every mathematics student's lonely struggle with difficult problems deep into the night—mathematics has always been an arduous and solitary journey.

A pure math seminar that I independently founded and organized

01/2025

In the winter of my junior year, I galvanized fellow students within the School of Mathematics and Physics who shared a passion for pure mathematics, creating a platform for us to share our knowledge. I single-handedly managed all speaker invitations and promotional efforts. My own role was to lecture on Galois theory, tracing its historical motivation to the unsolvability of the quintic by radicals. I was profoundly impressed by the sheer beauty of its symmetric structures and the undeniable genius of Galois himself. It was immensely rewarding to see the initiative receive widespread participation and strong support. Furthermore, the seminar successfully cultivated a cross-disciplinary learning environment, allowing participants to gain exposure to mathematical fields outside their own specialties.

My ideal mathematics

03/2025

When I heard that Professor Adam was organizing an undergraduate seminar on category theory, I immediately scoured the internet and the library for introductions and textbooks on the subject. The moment I grasped that category theory is "the discipline that studies all mathematical objects and the mappings between them as a unified whole. Its core focus is not on the 'internal' structure of objects, but on the 'relationships' between objects," I was thrilled. This was the mathematics of my ideals, perfectly aligning with my vision of mathematical unity and structural relations. I then realized that my previous understanding of the motivation behind group homomorphisms—"when a problem is difficult to solve in one context, we construct a bridge via homomorphisms to discuss it in another, more transparent context"—was merely a glimpse into the ideas of categories and functors! Without hesitation, I joined the seminar and proactively took on the role of a primary presenter. When the seminar progressed to representable functors and the Yoneda lemma, I couldn't help but marvel: "The Yoneda Lemma fundamentally reveals that an object is essentially uniquely determined by its relationships with all other objects." This was not merely a technical lemma; it was a profound philosophy: mathematical reality is not composed of isolated 'things,' but is defined by the 'network of interactions' between them. From that point on, I resolved to advance further on this ideal mathematical path of category theory. This decision also set the stage for my subsequent deep dive into algebraic topology and homotopy theory, where I would employ the language of categories to comprehend the grand landscape of topological spaces and homotopy invariants.

Student assistant in Help center

04/2025

My proficiency and passion in algebra have been widely recognized within the School. After serving as a Student Lecturer and Academic Navigator, I was appointed as an Algebra Teaching Assistant, responsible for supporting the teaching of second-year Abstract Algebra. I deeply valued this opportunity. Every week, I dedicated time at the School's Help Center, assisting younger students with their algebra questions and guiding them toward a solid understanding of abstract algebraic concepts. Additionally, I took the initiative to design and write up challenging problems, publishing them on the university's platform. It brought me immense satisfaction whenever a student would approach me, excited to share that they had successfully solved one of my puzzles.
